Most "Beeswax" Candles Aren't Pure Beeswax

There's no legal standard requiring a candle labeled "beeswax" to actually be 100% beeswax. A candle can be 10% beeswax ‚Äî blended with paraffin or soy ‚Äî and still call itself a beeswax candle on the label.

Paraffin is a petroleum byproduct. When burned, it releases volatile organic compounds including benzene and toluene ‚Äî the same compounds found in vehicle exhaust. Soy wax, while plant-based, is typically extracted using hexane solvents and is still often blended with paraffin.
